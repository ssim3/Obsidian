## Leg Positioning

### Keep foot tight to the hip/Keep active scissoring motion 

![[Pasted image 20250616183127.png]]

- Keeping top foot close to opponents hips makes it more difficult to strip the top leg
- Active tension by scissoring both legs in opposite directions allows for more control

## Off Balancing Opponent

- Use widening of base in conjunction with rotational force from top knee and arms to motivate opponent to keep hands on mat
### Keep top knee on opponents hamstrings

![[Pasted image 20250616183922.png]]

- Having knee behind opponents hamstrings allows for more rotational force into opponent
- [Lachlan Giles Open Guard](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1N0WHIdp3Fi7SL_87UaKPYP8Bjb4m5mOd/view?usp=sharing) (50:49)

### Use arms to create rotation through the leg

![[Pasted image 20250616184120.png]]

- Engage arms to twist leg to encourage opponent to put weight forward
- [Lachlan Giles Open Guard](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1N0WHIdp3Fi7SL_87UaKPYP8Bjb4m5mOd/view?usp=sharing) (51:30)

### Pushing legs apart allows for easier sweeps

![[Pasted image 20250616184521.png]]

- Keeping opponents base wide prevents him from adjusting his legs easily to stay standing
- [Lachlan Giles Open Guard](https://drive.google.com/file/d/1N0WHIdp3Fi7SL_87UaKPYP8Bjb4m5mOd/view?usp=sharing) (52:15)
